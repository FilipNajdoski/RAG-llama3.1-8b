The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald
Context: In the roaring 1920s, the story is narrated by Nick Carraway, a young man who moves to West Egg, Long Island, next door to the mysterious millionaire Jay Gatsby.
Plot Overview: The novel follows Gatsby's obsessive quest to win back his former lover, Daisy Buchanan, who is now married to the wealthy but cruel Tom Buchanan. Gatsby throws extravagant parties, hoping to attract Daisy, and eventually they begin an affair.
Key Themes: The story explores themes of the American Dream, social class, love, wealth, and deception. It contrasts old money with new money and highlights the moral decay hidden beneath a veneer of glamour and prosperity.
Resolution: The affair ends tragically when Daisy, driving Gatsby's car, accidentally kills Tom's mistress. Gatsby takes the blame to protect Daisy, leading to his own murder by the mistress's vengeful husband, who believes Gatsby was solely responsible. The novel concludes with Gatsby's lonely funeral, a stark commentary on the superficiality of his social circle.
